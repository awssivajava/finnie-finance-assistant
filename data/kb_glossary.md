## Financial glossary (selected terms)

- **Asset** – something you own that has value, such as cash, stocks, bonds, or property.
- **Liability** – something you owe, such as a loan or credit card balance.
- **Net worth** – your assets minus your liabilities.
- **Volatility** – how much the price of an investment moves up and down over time.
- **Index** – a collection of assets that represents a segment of the market, such as the S&P 500.
- **ETF (exchange-traded fund)** – a fund that trades on an exchange and typically tracks an index.
- **Expense ratio** – the annual fee charged by a fund, expressed as a percentage of assets.
- **Dividend** – a cash payment from a company or fund to shareholders, often from profits.

These definitions are simplified for education. Real products may be more complex; always read official documentation before investing.
