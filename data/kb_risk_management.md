## Risk management

Risk management is about understanding the possibility of loss and taking steps to reduce the chance or impact of that loss.

Common techniques include:

- **Diversification** – holding many different investments instead of concentrating in one stock or sector.
- **Asset allocation** – dividing your portfolio among asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash, etc.) based on your goals and risk tolerance.
- **Rebalancing** – periodically adjusting your portfolio back to target weights when markets move.
- **Emergency fund** – holding cash to cover short-term needs so you are not forced to sell investments at a bad time.

Your personal **risk tolerance** depends on:

- Your time horizon.
- Your income stability.
- Your emotional comfort level with seeing account values rise and fall.

Good risk management does not eliminate risk but makes risk more intentional and aligned with your goals.
